# Festivals & Events in Norway -- Explorer Product Prospects

Research compiled 2026-03-09. Focus: recurring events with multiple physical locations spread across a city/region where visitors need to navigate between venues.

---

## 1. OPEN HOUSE / OPEN STUDIOS EVENTS

### 1.1 Open House Oslo
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Architecture
- **Season:** September (2026: Sep 11-13)
- **Website:** https://openhouseoslo.no
- **Size:** Norway's largest architecture festival. Tens of thousands of visitors.
- **Map fit:** 50-100+ buildings open across the city. Visitors plan routes between locations spread from Frogner to Nesodden. Multi-day. Already uses a program with locations -- perfect Explorer use case.

### 1.2 Oslo Open
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Art / Open studios
- **Season:** April (2026: Apr 18-19)
- **Website:** https://osloopen.no
- **Size:** 400-450 open studios, est. 30,000+ visitors
- **Map fit:** Hundreds of studios spread across all Oslo districts. Visitors plan routes by neighborhood. Core navigation problem. Already has an artist directory but needs a map-first approach.

### 1.3 Trondheim Open
- **City:** Trondheim / Trondelag
- **Type:** Art / Open studios
- **Season:** Biennial (every 2 years), autumn
- **Website:** https://trondheimopen.org
- **Size:** 100+ studios across the region. Central Norway's largest contemporary art exhibition.
- **Map fit:** Studios spread across the entire Trondelag region, not just city center. Multi-day. Visitors need to plan routes across a large geographic area.

### 1.4 B-Open
- **City:** Bergen
- **Type:** Art / Open studios
- **Season:** September, annual
- **Website:** https://www.facebook.com/bopenartfestival/
- **Size:** Multiple studios + workshops across Bergen. Free admission.
- **Map fit:** Studios distributed across Bergen's neighborhoods. Visitors navigate between hillside ateliers and city center galleries. Bergen's topography makes route planning especially valuable.

### 1.5 Open Studios Stavanger
- **City:** Stavanger / Sandnes
- **Type:** Art / Open studios
- **Season:** Annual
- **Website:** http://www.openstudiosstavanger.com
- **Size:** Professional artists in Stavanger and Sandnes open their studios.
- **Map fit:** Two-city event (Stavanger + Sandnes). Visitors need to navigate between municipalities.

### 1.6 Oslo Architecture Triennale (OAT)
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Architecture / Design
- **Season:** Every 3 years, autumn (2026: opens Sep 17)
- **Website:** https://www.oslotriennale.no
- **Size:** Major international event. Multiple venues.
- **Map fit:** Exhibitions and events across National Museum, AHO, former Munch Museum, and other venues throughout Oslo. Multi-week duration.

## 2. ART FESTIVALS WITH MULTIPLE VENUES

### 2.1 Oslo Art Weekend
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Contemporary art
- **Season:** Late May (2026: May 28-31)
- **Website:** https://osloartweekend.no
- **Size:** 50-70+ art institutions participating. Four days.
- **Map fit:** Coordinated openings across museums, galleries, artist-run spaces, and public institutions across Oslo. The entire point is venue-hopping. Ideal Explorer use case.

### 2.2 Festspillene i Bergen (Bergen International Festival)
- **City:** Bergen
- **Type:** Multi-art (music, theatre, dance, opera, visual art)
- **Season:** Late May - early June (2026: May 27 - Jun 10)
- **Website:** https://www.fib.no
- **Size:** Nordic's largest arts festival. 14 days. Thousands of visitors.
- **Map fit:** Performances at Grieg Hall, Haakon's Hall, Troldhaugen, Lysoen, Siljustol, plus streets and squares throughout Bergen. Visitors need to navigate between very different locations across the city and surrounding area.

### 2.3 Lofoten International Art Festival (LIAF)
- **City:** Lofoten (various locations)
- **Type:** Contemporary art biennial
- **Season:** Biennial, autumn
- **Website:** https://www.nnks.no/en/lofoten-international-art-festival-liaf
- **Size:** Scandinavia's longest-running art biennial. Next: 2027.
- **Map fit:** Extremely map-dependent. Artworks installed in garages, libraries, sheds, bunkers, fish drying racks, private houses, shops, warehouses -- all spread across the Lofoten islands. No permanent venue. Visitors absolutely need navigation between remote sites.

### 2.4 Nuart Festival
- **City:** Stavanger
- **Type:** Street art
- **Season:** September, annual
- **Website:** https://www.nuartfestival.no
- **Size:** International street art festival since 2001. Part of Open House Worldwide network.
- **Map fit:** Murals and installations spread across Stavanger's walls, both indoor and outdoor. The street art trail is inherently map-based -- visitors walk routes between murals. Already has an informal trail concept.

### 2.5 Fjord Oslo (Light Art Festival)
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Light art / Public art
- **Season:** November (2025: Nov 6-9)
- **Website:** https://fjordoslo.com
- **Size:** Norway's biggest light art festival. Free outdoor exhibition.
- **Map fit:** Installations along Oslo's harbor promenade from Tjuvholmen to Aker Brygge to Radhusplassen. Visitors walk between light installations. Multi-location outdoor route.

### 2.6 Geiranger Lyskunstfestival
- **City:** Geiranger
- **Type:** Light art
- **Season:** Autumn
- **Website:** https://fjordgeiranger.no
- **Size:** Light installations across Geiranger village and surrounding nature.
- **Map fit:** Light installations on buildings and in nature around the fjord. Small village but installations spread across landscape. Visitors navigate in darkness.

## 3. MUSIC FESTIVALS WITH MULTIPLE VENUES/STAGES

### 3.1 by:Larm
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Music showcase + conference
- **Season:** September/October, annual
- **Website:** https://bylarm.no
- **Size:** 100+ artists, 10+ venues, 3 days. Nordic's largest music conference.
- **Map fit:** Classic multi-venue city festival. Visitors hop between Bla, Revolver, Kulturkirken Jakob, and 7+ other venues across central Oslo. Scheduling + navigation is the core user problem. Perfect Explorer fit.

### 3.2 Trondheim Calling
- **City:** Trondheim
- **Type:** Music showcase + conference
- **Season:** January/February, annual
- **Website:** https://trondheimcalling.no
- **Size:** 90 concerts, 60 artists, 9 venues, 3 days.
- **Map fit:** Multi-venue city showcase. "9 scener" is literally their tagline. Visitors navigate between downtown venues. Same multi-venue challenge as by:Larm.

### 3.3 Musikkfest Oslo
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Music (all genres)
- **Season:** June (2026: Jun 6)
- **Website:** https://musikkfest.no
- **Size:** 50+ outdoor stages, hundreds of artists, free. One day.
- **Map fit:** Extraordinary map need: 50+ stages in parks, squares, and streets across the entire city. Free concerts from morning to night. Visitors need real-time navigation between dozens of locations. One of the strongest use cases on this list.

### 3.4 Ultima Contemporary Music Festival
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Contemporary/experimental music
- **Season:** September-October (2026: Sep 10-19)
- **Website:** https://ultima.no
- **Size:** Nordic's largest contemporary music festival. 10 days.
- **Map fit:** Events at Opera House, Concert Hall, National Museum, plus "unexpected urban spaces." Multi-venue, multi-day, spread across the city.

### 3.5 Oslo World
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** World music / Culture
- **Season:** Late October - early November (2026: Oct 27 - Nov 1)
- **Website:** https://www.osloworld.no
- **Size:** Major festival across 10+ venues. Founded 1994.
- **Map fit:** Uses Sentrum Scene, Cosmopolite, Parkteatret, Victoria, MUNCH, Kulturkirken Jakob, Riksscenen, Oslo Konserthus, Gronland Kulturstasjon. Visitors navigate between many different venues across central/east Oslo.

### 3.6 Kongsberg Jazzfestival
- **City:** Kongsberg
- **Type:** Jazz
- **Season:** July, annual
- **Website:** https://kongsbergjazz.no
- **Size:** One of Europe's most renowned jazz festivals. Since 1964.
- **Map fit:** Multiple venues from intimate Gallery Akern (70 people) to Kirketorget main stage (11,000). All venues within walking distance in Kongsberg center. Already has venue/map page.

### 3.7 Nattjazz
- **City:** Bergen
- **Type:** Jazz
- **Season:** Late May - early June, annual
- **Website:** https://www.nattjazz.no
- **Size:** Northern Europe's longest jazz festival. ~80 concerts.
- **Map fit:** Main venue at USF Verftet (old sardine factory) but includes concerts, exhibitions, theatre, cinema across multiple spaces. Multi-week duration.

### 3.8 Moldejazz
- **City:** Molde
- **Type:** Jazz
- **Season:** July (week 29), annual
- **Website:** https://www.moldejazz.no
- **Size:** ~120 concerts, 500+ artists. Since 1961.
- **Map fit:** The city transforms into a festival arena. Multiple stages and venues across Molde. Week-long.

### 3.9 MaiJazz
- **City:** Stavanger
- **Type:** Jazz
- **Season:** May, annual
- **Website:** https://maijazz.no
- **Size:** 40 concerts on 20 venues. 200 volunteers.
- **Map fit:** 20 venues in and around Stavanger. Strong multi-venue distribution.

### 3.10 Sildajazz
- **City:** Haugesund
- **Type:** Jazz
- **Season:** August (second weekend), annual
- **Website:** https://sildajazz.no
- **Size:** 70+ orchestras, 400 musicians, 180+ events, 25 venues.
- **Map fit:** 25 venues across Haugesund. Extraordinary venue density for a small city. Strong map need.

### 3.11 DølaJazz
- **City:** Lillehammer
- **Type:** Jazz
- **Season:** October, annual
- **Website:** https://www.dolajazz.no
- **Size:** Multiple venues including churches, clubs, microbreweries.
- **Map fit:** Venues spread across Lillehammer including historic churches and cozy clubs.

### 3.12 Nordlysfestivalen (Northern Lights Festival)
- **City:** Tromso
- **Type:** Classical music / Multi-genre
- **Season:** January-February (2026: Jan 29 - Feb 7)
- **Website:** https://www.nordlysfestivalen.no
- **Size:** Established 1987. 10 days. Multiple genre from early music to jazz.
- **Map fit:** Multiple concert venues across Tromso during the polar night.

### 3.13 Notodden Blues Festival
- **City:** Notodden
- **Type:** Blues
- **Season:** August (first weekend), annual
- **Website:** https://bluesfest.no
- **Size:** Europe's largest / Scandinavia's largest blues festival. Since 1988.
- **Map fit:** Multiple stages across the small town of Notodden. Visitors navigate between indoor and outdoor venues.

### 3.14 Festspillene i Nord-Norge (Arctic Arts Festival)
- **City:** Harstad
- **Type:** Multi-art (music, theatre, dance, visual art)
- **Season:** June (2026: Jun 20-27)
- **Website:** https://festspillnn.no
- **Size:** 500 artists, 100 arrangements, 8 days.
- **Map fit:** Events at Trondenes Church, Nordic Hall, Harstad Church, bars, festival tent on quayside, Cultural House. Multiple venues across the town.

### 3.15 Vossa Jazz
- **City:** Voss
- **Type:** Jazz / Folk / World music
- **Season:** March (2026: Mar 27-29)
- **Website:** https://vossajazz.no
- **Size:** Established festival combining jazz, folk, and world music.
- **Map fit:** Multiple venues across Voss, a small town. Combined with Ekstremsportveko area.

## 4. FOOD & DRINK FESTIVALS

### 4.1 Gladmat
- **City:** Stavanger
- **Type:** Food
- **Season:** Late June / Late July (varies)
- **Website:** https://gladmat.no
- **Size:** Scandinavia's largest food festival. Along Vagen harbor.
- **Map fit:** Multiple areas along the harbor and beyond. Large festival with many food stands and venues spread along the waterfront.

### 4.2 Trøndelag Matfestival (Trondheim Food Festival)
- **City:** Trondheim
- **Type:** Food
- **Season:** August (2026: Aug 6-8)
- **Website:** https://oimat.no
- **Size:** ~200 exhibitors from 14 regions. Major food event.
- **Map fit:** Large festival area in Trondheim with many exhibitors. Visitors navigate between different food zones and regions.

### 4.3 Matstreif
- **City:** Oslo (Youngstorget)
- **Type:** Food & drink
- **Season:** September (2026: Sep 18-19)
- **Website:** https://matstreif.no
- **Size:** Norway's largest food and drink festival. 100+ exhibitors.
- **Map fit:** Centered on Youngstorget but with 100+ exhibitors to navigate between.

### 4.4 Bergen Matfestival
- **City:** Bergen
- **Type:** Food
- **Season:** September (2026: Sep 2-4)
- **Website:** https://matfest.no
- **Size:** Multiple areas including Festplassen, restaurant menus across the city, cider park.
- **Map fit:** Festplassen main area + participating restaurants throughout Bergen with special festival menus + separate cider park. Multi-location.

### 4.5 Norsk Rakfiskfestival
- **City:** Fagernes, Valdres
- **Type:** Food (traditional)
- **Season:** November (2026: Nov 5-7)
- **Website:** https://www.valdres.com
- **Size:** 25,000+ visitors. One of Norway's largest food festivals.
- **Map fit:** Multiple arenas in Fagernes center: main tent, Lavvoen, Rakfisk tent, food market, concert areas.

### 4.6 Bergen Whisky & Beer Festival
- **City:** Bergen
- **Type:** Beer & whisky
- **Season:** February, annual (2026: two weekends)
- **Website:** https://en.visitbergen.com/whats-on/bergen-olfestival-2026-p8220163
- **Size:** Norway's largest beer/whisky festival. Two weekends in 2026.
- **Map fit:** Multiple tasting areas plus brewery/distillery visits around the city.

## 5. KULTURNATT (CULTURE NIGHT) EVENTS

### 5.1 Oslo Kulturnatt
- **City:** Oslo
- **Type:** Multi-cultural (music, art, performances, exhibitions)
- **Season:** September (2025: Sep 12; 20th anniversary)
- **Website:** https://www.oslokulturnatt.no
- **Size:** Norway's largest one-day festival. 250+ events at 200+ venues. Free.
- **Map fit:** ULTIMATE Explorer use case. 200+ venues across Oslo, all in one evening. Visitors must navigate between events with limited time. Already uses a map/program but needs real-time routing and personalized recommendations.

### 5.2 Kulturnatt Trondheim
- **City:** Trondheim
- **Type:** Multi-cultural
- **Season:** September (2026: Sep 11)
- **Website:** https://kulturnatt-trondheim.no
- **Size:** Trondheim's biggest one-day festival. Free.
- **Map fit:** Concerts, shows, workshops, exhibitions across the city in one evening. Same navigation challenge as Oslo Kulturnatt at smaller scale.

## 6. HERITAGE / CITY WALK EVENTS

### 6.1 Kulturminnedagene (European Heritage Days Norway)
- **City:** All of Norway (hundreds of local events)
- **Season:** September, annual
- **Website:** https://kulturvern.no/kulturminnedagene/
- **Size:** Hundreds of events nationwide. Guided tours, open historic buildings, exhibitions.
- **Map fit:** Multiple open historic buildings and guided tours per city/region. Each local area has its own cluster of events that visitors navigate between.

### 6.2 Rørosmartnan (Roros Winter Market)
- **City:** Roros (UNESCO World Heritage)
- **Type:** Winter market / Cultural festival
- **Season:** February (2026: Feb 17-21)
- **Website:** https://rorosmartnan.no
- **Size:** 70,000 visitors annually. Since 1854. National event.
- **Map fit:** Events spread across the entire UNESCO World Heritage town center. Concerts, dancing, street performances, food stalls, horse/sleigh rides at various locations. Visitors navigate historic streets.

### 6.3 Julemarked Roros (Christmas Market)
- **City:** Roros
- **Type:** Christmas market
- **Season:** December (2026: Dec 3-6)
- **Website:** https://julemarkedroros.no
- **Size:** Voted Norway's best Christmas market. Four days.
- **Map fit:** Multiple stall areas and activities spread across the historic town center.

## KEY PATTERNS FOR SALES APPROACH

**Strongest signals for Explorer product-market fit:**
- Events where organizers already provide maps (by:Larm, Designers' Saturday, Kongsberg Jazz)
- Events with "venue-hopping" as core activity (Kulturnatt, Open studios, by:Larm, Trondheim Calling)
- Events spread across a city rather than contained in one venue
- Events where time management matters (one-day events like Kulturnatt, Musikkfest)
- Events with 10+ venues or 50+ points of interest

**Business model angles:**
- White-label Explorer for festival organizers (replace PDF maps)
- Sponsor integration (restaurants, venues can promote themselves to festival-goers)
- Analytics: which venues/areas get traffic, popular routes
- Pre-festival planning + live day-of navigation
- Post-festival: permanent trails (e.g., Nuart street art trail stays year-round)
